What the Project Says About Fairness

“Measuring Fairness” delivers a subtle but powerful argument: fairness is not a singular concept that can be computed objectively. Instead, it’s a sociotechnical judgment call embedded in every design choice.

Critical Interpretation

Although the explorable avoids moral prescriptions, it surfaces how algorithmic fairness is always political. It exposes the fallacy of neutral computation and gives viewers tools to think critically about how fairness is operationalized.

Appraisal and Critique

The project is laudable for its accessibility and clarity. However, by abstracting away real-world identities and using simulated data, it avoids the messiness of justice in actual deployment contexts. Additionally, its neutrality may reflect institutional caution more than genuine indeterminacy.
