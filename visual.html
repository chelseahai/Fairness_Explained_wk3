Design Strategies

The project adopts the visual language of “explorable explanations,” popularized by Bret Victor and Nicky Case. These designs encourage learning through interaction rather than static reading.

Stylistic Precedents

“Measuring Fairness” uses minimal colors, clear typography, and smooth transitions to ensure focus remains on the evolving metrics. Its visual clarity is aligned with practices found in Distill.pub and data journalism sites like The Pudding.

Persuasive Visuals

Rather than illustrate data passively, the project uses visuals to argue a point: that fairness is conditional. Sliders demonstrate trade-offs in real time, and user control reinforces the argument that fairness is not absolute, but negotiable.
