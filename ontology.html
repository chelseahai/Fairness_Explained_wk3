What Exists in the Project

The “Measuring Fairness” project is built as an explorable explanation — an interactive digital tool that illustrates the complexity of fairness in algorithmic systems.

It consists of multiple conceptual and interactive elements, including:

  • Fairness metrics such as demographic parity, equalized odds, and predictive parity.
  • Trade-offs between metrics that are visualized through interactive elements.
  • A fabricated dataset to simplify and clarify conceptual conflicts.
  • Sliders and toggles to manipulate thresholds and examine consequences.
  • Graphical outputs such as confusion matrices, ROC curves, and class distributions.
  • A pedagogical narrative guiding users step-by-step through fairness definitions.

Modular Architecture

Each module of the explorable is logically discrete, corresponding to different fairness concepts. The architecture reinforces the notion that fairness is made of multiple, often incompatible logics, and users are invited to explore them separately and in contrast.
