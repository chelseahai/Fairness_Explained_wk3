Historical Context

Released in 2018, “Measuring Fairness” emerged in a climate of growing concern over algorithmic bias. The public was increasingly aware of how AI systems could reinforce systemic inequities.

  • In 2016, ProPublica revealed racial bias in COMPAS, a criminal risk prediction algorithm.
  • In 2018, Joy Buolamwini and Timnit Gebru exposed racial disparities in facial recognition performance.
  • Explainable AI (XAI) became a major research focus during this time.

Authorial Intent and Audience

The PAIR team sought to clarify fairness trade-offs in a visually digestible way, creating a tool to help both experts and laypeople understand that fairness in ML is not absolute. The audience includes engineers, data scientists, students, policymakers, and journalists.
